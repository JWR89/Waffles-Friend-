He Walks With Me Here

“Come rest,” says the Grove where the lanterns glow bright,
“There’s peace in the stillness and joy in the light.”
As I sit in the hush with the breeze in my ear,
I smile and remember… He walks with me here.

“Come pray,” hums the Tree with its bark carved in grace,
“Leave every worry in this sacred place.”
I whisper my hopes, my hurts disappear—
And deep in my spirit… He walks with me here.

“Come hear,” says the Chapel where scripture is strong,
“Declare what is right, and stand against wrong.”
The truth makes me brave, I feel no fear—
Because I know this… He walks with me here.

“Come be changed,” breathes the Meadow as dusk fills the sky,
His love gently shaping the tears that I cry.
Tomorrow may wait, for His mercy is near—
My story finds hope… for He walks with me here.
